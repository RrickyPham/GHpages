Chapter 16: How does it all work?

The conjecture itself is pretty simple. You start with a number, in our case: 3, if this number is odd then you multiply it by 3 and add 1. That is your new number. If your number is even, you divide it by 2.

But here's the kicker. The question: "Is there any starting number where the sequence does not end up in a loop?" a circle, as it were. You can do the math, you know how this will end, don't you? But does that rob us of the experience, the "right now"?

Chapter 1: Death

In the end, it ends.

The Collatz Conjecture will not survive the death of the sun. That big flaming circle in the sky will eventually not have enough internal pressure to stave off the effects of gravity. It will collapse, and then begin to fuse heavier elements in its core- kickstarting an expansion that will envelop the earth. In the same way it provides the low entropy needed for life to exist, it will also be what brings life out. We all lose the battle against entropy.

But you should know by now that there are no "ends" nor "begginings", just moving forward. Are you brave enough to live again?
